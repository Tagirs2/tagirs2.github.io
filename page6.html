Who this article is for?

You must be reading this because you've been contemplating having your eyebrows done. This is a giant step to take and one that needs to be done wisely. Until I began my career down this path, I never fathomed how having brows was so personal for so many people.
I love to listen to each and every one of my clients
share with me stories on how they've gotten to the point that they're sitting in my chair.

Some have severe health problems that made them lose their brows.
Some have Trichotillomania and I have one been one of the very few
entrusted along with their family that knows they have this problem.
Some were born with lots of brows, but are so light they look non-existent.
And some of us, (myself included) were just victims of bad faddish 90's over-plucking and they never came back.
Whatever your reasoning this will help you to be packed with more general knowledge of the procedure. 

What is microblading?

Microblading is the Word of the Day, but what many don't know is that not everyone is a candidate for this.
The manual blade is acupuncture sized needles aligned to form a row. It's feather-thin and ultra-sharp. Its then placed into a disposable wand and the technician glides this implement superficially along your brow line. Your artist will create tiny cuts in the process and pigment will fall down the blade into this light scratch. Precision and exact skill is required for every hair stroke. I always make sure I follow my clients natural brow pattern so the newly placed stroke will blend seamlessly.

Microblading is incredible looking and even to this day I'm blown away by how realistic this looks......at first. 

Choose the right artist and the right pigment

This is where having a good artist AND someone trained in knowledge of skin is so important. Clients with larger pores, oily skin or very thin skin are not good candidates. What will look beautiful at first, will not heal beautiful. The pigment will blur and not resemble a hairstroke any longer. Clients need to have drier skin and smooth texture.

power of powder brows

Powderbrows are less talked about, but almost every person is a good candidate for this. It's done by a machine method.
When people think of getting their brows done by machine their mind automatically races to the tattoo-parlor type brows and they worry they'll look like they just were released from prison.......times have changed!!!
The torque in these permanent makeup machines is set differently so the impact is much lighter and very gentle. The needle hits your skin as tiny little dots and is built up until your desired look is achieved. Varying techniques can give you differing looks but the healed results will be light and dusty with a makeup effect and generally this method lasts longer than the traditional Microbladed method.

One thing to always remember, and I hear this misconception far too often.....Microblading is NOT semi-permanent. This is a tattoo and that molecule will always be in your skin. This makes choosing the right artist so incredibly important.

View this as a marriage and this long-term commitment must be done with the perfect partner. If the wrong decision is made it will be costly, depressing and painful.

Choose the artist that when you see their work your heart swoons inside and you know they're the one for you.
