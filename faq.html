FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ABOUT MICROBLADING, OMBRE BROWS, NANO BROWS AND COMBO BROWS

1. Am I eligible for a brow service? 

You are ineligible for service if you are: pregnant, breast feeding, diabetic, have glaucoma, super thin skin, skin disease or Eczema, Rosacea, post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, heavy acne near on in the brow area, HIV/Hepatitis, are under 18, prone to keloids, undergoing active chemotherapy or taking skin medications like oral steroids. Please reach out directly if you are unsure whether you are eligible.

2. How do I get ready for my appointment? 

6 months prior: Must be off Accutane and any Chemotherepy treatments/medications. 

30 days prior: No Botox or direct sun/UV exposure or burn to the face 30 days before or after treatment 

2-weeks prior: Discontinue use of exfoliating products such as AHA’s (Salicylic, Glycolic, Lactic acid) and Retinol products. No tweezing, plucking to waxing. No facials or microdermabrasion. 

72 hours prior: No Fish Oil supplements, Advil/Ibuprofen or Aspirin. No use of Vitamin E or Niacin.  

48 hours prior: No alcohol 

24 hour prior: No caffeine  

3. How to I take care of my brows post-appointment?  

Your skin is vulnerable to what you put on your face, so please wash your hands before treating the area. You are expected to wash your brows morning and night for 10 days. I will provide you with a cleanser and healing balm for optimal results. Itching, flaking and scabbing is normal. DO NOT pick, peel or scratch the area. This may result in uneven healing, scarring and/or infection. No makeup (specifically eyeshadow/foundation) is to be worn for up to one week as products can migrate and irritate/infect the brows. Do not expose your healing skin to direct sun, tanning beds, saunas, salt water, chlorinated pools, direct shower spray, skin creams/serums, sunscreen or anything else you’ve been instructed not to use for 10 days. Lastly, do not do any heavy workouts for up to one week; sweat will ash out the color. If you plan on vacationing to a tropical destination, please try to schedule your appointment, two weeks before or after. 

4. Is PMU a real tattoo? 

A machine is used to deposit pigment by gently shading the surface of the skin (upper layer of the dermis, just below the epidermis). This IS a tattoo, however, traditional tattooing implants pigment into a deeper layer of skin allowing pigment to last much longer than it would with Permanent Makeup (PMU). 

5. What pigment line do you use?  

I use Permanent Blend pigments, or variations thereof (Tina Davies / Brow Daddy). These pigments are Gamma-ray sterilized to industry standards, Vegan and Cruelty-Free. They are made of pigment, glycerin, witch hazel, aqua, rosin, benzyl alcohol, and isopropyl alcohol. These pigments have a high-retention of 80-100%. 

6. How long does it last? 

Your brows are meant to last between 1-3 years depending on your skin genetics, lifestyle and other environmental factors. Yearly touchups are recommended to maintain shape and color.

7. What is the difference between Microblading and Ombré shading? 

I DO NOT offer Microblading. This is a technique that uses a nano blade to etch hair-like strokes into the skin. Ombré or Powder brows are meant to mimic a soft, natural makeup look by depositing pigment with a machine that gently shades the surface of the skin. It is less invasive and great for all skin-types including oily or mature. Combo Brows are a combination of the two techniques; 95% shading & 5% Microblading at the head of the brow. I currently offer both Ombré (Powder) and Combo Brows.
